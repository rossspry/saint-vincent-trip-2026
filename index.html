Emergency & important info

Plain-language emergency contacts and radio scripts for any crew member, including teens, if the captain or adults are injured or away from the boat. Confirm phone numbers with Barefoot at checkout.

First action in danger

  1. Put on life jackets.
  2. Call on VHF Channel 16.
  3. If life is in immediate danger, say MAYDAY MAYDAY MAYDAY.
  4. If urgent but not immediately life-threatening, say PAN PAN PAN PAN PAN PAN.
  5. Give boat name: Inconceivable, MMSI 368392220.
  6. Give location from chartplotter, phone GPS, AIS page, or nearest island/anchorage.

Call words explained

MAYDAY
Use only for grave and imminent danger: sinking, fire, person overboard, severe injury, abandon ship, or life in immediate danger.
PAN PAN
Urgent help needed, but no immediate danger to life or the boat: disabled engine, medical problem, lost, drifting, or non-life-threatening injury.
SECURITÉ
Safety information for other vessels: hazard, floating debris, navigation warning, or dangerous condition.

What to read from the boat

  • Boat name: Inconceivable
  • MMSI: 368392220
  • Position: chartplotter GPS, phone GPS, or nearest island/anchorage
  • People aboard: 7
  • Nature of problem: medical, fire, flooding, aground, adrift, person overboard
  • What help is needed: medical advice, evacuation, tow, rescue, coast guard

If you are a teenager or inexperienced crew

  1. Do not panic. Speak slowly.
  2. Press VHF Channel 16 or use the red distress button only if life is in danger.
  3. Say the script exactly. It is okay to read it.
  4. Keep the radio on. Listen for instructions.
  5. If using a phone, call 911, 999, or 112 and say you are on a boat in St. Vincent and the Grenadines.
  6. Send one person to watch for other boats or help.
